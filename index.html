Core Philosophy Index

The Three Pillars of Interpretation

Guruji explains the Names through three distinct traditional lens:

  • Advaita: Seeing the Lord as the non-dual ultimate reality.
  • Viśiṣṭādvaita: Exploring the relationship between the body and soul (Śarīra-Śarīrī).
  • Dvaita: Understanding the eternal service of the individual soul to the Supreme.

IAST Pronunciation Guide

Ensuring Vedic Accuracy

Vedic chanting requires precise phonetics. Key rules used in this syllabus:

Vowels: ā, ī, ū are long; e, o are always long in Sanskrit.

Dots Below: ṇ, ṣ, ṭ, ḍ signify retroflex sounds (tongue curled back).

The Visarga: ḥ acts as a soft echo of the previous vowel.
